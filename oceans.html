The five oceans

From surface to abyss

Each ocean has its own character, its own depths, and its own wonders. Dive in and discover what lies beneath.

Pacific Ocean
165.25M km²Area
10,935 mDeepest Point

The largest and deepest ocean on Earth. The Mariana Trench plunges deeper than Everest is tall. It holds more than half of all the world's ocean water.

Atlantic Ocean
106.46M km²Area
8,376 mDeepest Point

The ocean that connects the Old World and the New. The Mid-Atlantic Ridge — the longest mountain range on Earth — runs along its floor.

Indian Ocean
70.56M km²Area
7,258 mDeepest Point

The warmest of the world's oceans. Ancient mariners navigated its monsoon winds for millennia to trade spices, silk, and gold across continents.

Southern Ocean
21.96M km²Area
7,236 mDeepest Point

The newest named ocean, encircling Antarctica. The Antarctic Circumpolar Current is the planet's largest ocean current, driving global climate systems.

Arctic Ocean
14.06M km²Area
5,550 mDeepest Point

The smallest and shallowest ocean, covered by sea ice for much of the year. Polar bears, narwhals, and the midnight sun call this frozen frontier home.
